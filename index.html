Abstract

We present an LLM-powered virtual guide for rupestrian churches in Matera. The system integrates speech transcription, visual object detection, spatial contextualization, and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) to deliver context-aware responses grounded in curated cultural heritage knowledge.

Beyond system implementation, we introduce a controlled experimental testbed for evaluating the causal impact of retrieval augmentation on response quality, grounding fidelity, robustness, and stability. A paired RAG ON/OFF design is adopted across multiple large-scale language models (70B–120B parameters), enabling controlled within-query comparisons under identical prompting conditions.

Evaluation combines automated retrieval-aware metrics (RAGAS) with blind expert assessments conducted by specialists in medieval archaeology.
